Industrial Machine Costs at a Glance

Anyone evaluating investment based only on purchase price risks higher long-term costs.

1. Acquisition Costs

Machine, base equipment, and technical add-ons define your starting investment.

2. Integration Costs

Transport, installation, commissioning, and training should be included from day one.

3. Operating Costs

Energy usage, consumables, and operator effort influence cost per part every day.

4. Maintenance and Availability

Downtime costs are often the most expensive factor - service capability is critical.

5. Quality Costs

Scrap, rework, and complaints directly impact your margins and delivery performance.

Conclusion

Compare solutions based on total cost over the planned lifecycle, not only on purchase price.
